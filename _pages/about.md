I am a physics and computer science graduate from Université de Montréal, interested in quantum information, scientific computing, and the foundations of computation.

My work sits at the intersection of physics, programming, and computer science. I am especially interested in how computational tools can help us model, simulate, and reason about complex scientific systems.

I have built interactive simulations for teaching special relativity, including VPython visualizations of Lorentz contraction, Minkowski diagrams, and Michelson interferometry. I have also worked on quantum information projects such as CHSH Bell inequality simulations, E91 quantum key distribution, and quantum-inspired game mechanics.

Research interests
======

- Quantum information and quantum cryptography
- Relativistic protocols and zero-knowledge proofs
- Scientific computing and simulation
- Foundations of computation
- Programming languages, semantics, and formal methods
- Computational physics and biophysics

Selected projects
======

### Interactive relativity simulations

I developed VPython-based simulations to help visualize concepts in special relativity, including Lorentz transformations, length contraction, and reference-frame-dependent geometry.

### CHSH Bell inequality simulation

I implemented a simulation of the CHSH Bell inequality test using Qiskit, including Bell-state preparation, randomized measurement bases, correlation calculations, and visualization of results.

### E91 quantum key distribution

I worked on an implementation of the E91 quantum key distribution protocol using entangled Bell pairs, CHSH testing, key extraction, and message encryption/decryption.

### Quantum Battleship

I designed a quantum-themed Battleship game exploring ideas inspired by measurement, collapse, and entanglement.

### Computational biophysics

During a biophysics research internship, I worked on computational analysis related to cellular systems and physical modeling.

Teaching
======

I have experience tutoring mathematics and contributing to physics education. I have also worked as a teaching assistant for a special relativity course, where I developed interactive tools to support students’ intuition.
